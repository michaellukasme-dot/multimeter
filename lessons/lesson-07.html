Continuity & Diode Test

Continuity is the fastest circuit check you have — a beep confirms a connected path in under a second. The diode test verifies semiconductor junctions without a separate component.

)))
Continuity
Beeps when resistance <~50Ω. Circuit must be de-energized. Tests fuses, switches, wires, PCB traces.
Beep ≈ <50Ω path
⊣
Diode Test
Applies ~2V. Shows forward voltage drop. Tests diodes, LEDs, and BJT junctions.
Si diode: 0.5V – 0.7V
Interpreting Results
🔊 BEEP
Connected
Good wire, closed switch, healthy fuse, or two PCB pads that should be linked.
🔇 Silent
Open circuit
Broken wire, blown fuse, open switch, or disconnected pad.
🔊 Unexpected
Unwanted short
Two traces that should be isolated are connected — solder bridge, pinched wire.
Example 1 — Fuse Test
Continuity
  1. Remove fuse from holder (never test in-circuit with power on).
  2. Set to continuity mode ))).
  3. One probe to each end of fuse.
  4. BEEP = good fuse. Silent / OL = blown fuse, replace it.
Example 2 — Diode Forward Voltage
Diode
  1. Set to diode symbol ⊣.
  2. Red probe to diode Anode (+), black to Cathode (−) (banded end).
  3. Read forward voltage. Reverse probes — should show OL.
Silicon:0.5V–0.7V Red LED:1.8V–2.2V Blue/White LED:3.0V–3.4V
