AC Voltage (V~)

Alternating current reverses direction at 60 Hz (North America) or 50 Hz (Europe). It's what powers your home and is significantly more dangerous than typical DC sources.

V~
AC Voltage
Measures mains power, transformer outputs, and AC signals. Displays RMS value.
Ranges: 200V / 600V / 750V
RMS
True-RMS
Higher-end meters accurately measure non-sinusoidal waveforms (motor drives, inverters). Budget meters assume pure sine wave.
Important for VFDs & UPS
⚡
DANGER — Mains voltage kills. 120V/240V AC is lethal. Use CAT III rated probes, keep fingers behind guard rings, never work alone on live circuits, and turn the breaker off whenever possible.

Practical Examples
Example 1 — Wall Outlet (120V USA)
High Voltage
  1. Set to V~, 200V range or higher.
  2. Red into shorter (hot) slot, black into longer (neutral) slot.
  3. Expected: 110V – 125V
  4. Move black to ground (round) hole — should read same as hot-to-neutral.
⚠
Reading below 105V suggests high resistance in the wiring. Reading 0V means dead outlet — check for tripped GFCI or breaker.
Example 2 — Transformer Output
AC Signal
  1. Set to V~, choose range above expected output.
  2. Probe across secondary winding terminals.
  3. A 12V AC transformer should read 11V – 13V under load.
  4. Compare to label rating — more than 10% deviation suggests winding fault or overload.
