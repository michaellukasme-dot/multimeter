What is an Electric Multimeter?

A multimeter is the single most useful tool in any electrician's, engineer's, or hobbyist's toolkit — it measures voltage, current, resistance, and more from one handheld device.

💡
Why learn this? Understanding your multimeter turns guesswork into precision. Whether you're troubleshooting a car battery, wiring a home circuit, or debugging an Arduino project, a multimeter gives you ground truth.
The Multimeter at a Glance
LCD Display
Shows measured value, unit, and mode. Modern meters auto-range and show polarity.
Rotary Selector Dial
Switches between modes: DC voltage, AC voltage, current, resistance, continuity, and more.
Test Lead Ports
Three ports: COM (black), VΩmA (red, everyday), 10A (red, high current only).
HOLD Button
Freezes the reading — useful when you can't see the display while probing.
RANGE Button
Fixes the measurement scale manually, overriding auto-range.

Multimeters come in two types: analog (needle + scale) and digital (LCD readout). This course focuses on digital multimeters (DMMs) — more accurate, easier to read, and the modern standard.

⚡
Safety first — always. Never measure current by placing probes in parallel. Never exceed the meter's rated voltage (usually 600V–1000V CAT III). Use insulated probes and keep fingers behind the guard rings.
Quick Reference: What Can It Measure?

Voltage
DC (batteries, electronics) and AC (mains, transformers).
A / mA
Current
How much charge flows — LEDs, motors, battery drain.
Ω
Resistance
Opposition to current — resistors, windings, wire.

Signal frequency and capacitor value (advanced meters).
