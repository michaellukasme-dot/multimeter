Lab: Battery Testing

Batteries behave differently under load. Open-circuit voltage can look healthy while the battery fails under real load. This lab covers comprehensive battery assessment across all common types.

Car Battery — Full 4-Step Diagnostic
Automotive 12V Battery Procedure
Lab Exercise
  1. Static test: Ignition OFF. Set V⎓ 20V. Red → + terminal, black → −. Record resting voltage.
  2. Load test: Turn on headlights. Re-measure. Must stay above 11.5V — if not, battery capacity is failing.
  3. Cranking test: While someone cranks the engine, watch voltage. Must stay above 9.6V. Lower = weak battery or corroded cable.
  4. Charging check: Engine running. Measure battery terminals. Should read 13.7V – 14.7V — alternator charging. Outside this = alternator problem.
Li-Ion Cell Assessment (e.g. 18650)
Lab Exercise
  1. Set V⎓, 20V range. Red → positive end, black → negative end.
  2. Full charge: 4.1V – 4.2V   Storage OK: 3.6V – 3.9V
  3. Below 3.0V: over-discharged — may be damaged
  4. Never charge a Li-Ion below 2.5V — risk of internal short and thermal runaway.
