Anatomy & Components

Before you take a single measurement, know your tool. Each port, button, and dial marking has a specific purpose — using the wrong one can damage the meter or create a hazard.

Test Lead Ports
10A
High-Current Input
Red probe for currents above 200mA only. No fuse protection — time-limited to 30 seconds.
⚠ Max 10A
VΩmA
Main Input
Red probe for voltage, resistance, capacitance, continuity, diode, and low current. Your everyday port.
Fused — most measurements
COM
Common / Ground
Black probe always goes here. This is the reference for all measurements. Never swap probes in this port.
Always black lead
⚠
Probe condition matters. Cracked insulation, exposed wire, or bent pins are shock hazards. Inspect before every use — replace damaged leads immediately.
Test Lead Anatomy

Guard rings prevent fingers from sliding onto the metal tip — never probe without them

Reading the Display
12.34
Normal reading
Value is within range. Unit shown on right (V, mA, Ω, etc.).
OL
Overload
Reading exceeds range. Switch to higher range or let auto-range select.
−5.2
Negative polarity
On DC, red probe is at lower potential than black. Swap probes or note reversed polarity.
0.000
Zero / continuity
For resistance: dead short. For voltage: no potential difference across measurement points.
CAT Safety Ratings

All meters and probes carry a CAT (Category) rating that defines what environments they are safe to use in:

RatingUse CaseMax Transient
CAT ILow-energy circuits, signal levels800V
CAT IISingle-phase receptacles, portable tools1000V
CAT IIIDistribution panels, fixed installations2500V
CAT IVUtility service entrance, outdoor lines4000V
💡
For general home electrical work, a CAT III 600V meter and matching probes is the minimum safe standard. Never use a lower-rated meter in a higher-risk environment.
