DC Voltage (V⎓)

Direct current flows in one direction — from positive to negative. It powers batteries, electronics, and automotive systems, and is the most common measurement you'll take.

V⎓
DC Voltage
Measures steady-state voltage in batteries, DC power supplies, and electronic circuits.
Ranges: 200mV / 2V / 20V / 200V / 600V
AUTO
Auto-Range
Most meters auto-select the best range. On manual meters, start highest and work down.
Recommended for beginners
Connection Diagram — DC Voltage (Parallel)
💡
Key rule: Voltage is always measured in parallel — red to + side, black to − side. The meter's ~10MΩ input impedance means it barely affects the circuit.
Practical Examples
Example 1 — Testing a 9V Battery
DC Voltage
  1. Turn dial to V⎓.
  2. Red probe → VΩmA port, black probe → COM port.
  3. Red probe to battery + terminal, black to − terminal.
  4. Read the display.
Healthy:8.5V – 9.5V Weak:7.0V – 8.4V Dead:below 7V
Example 2 — Arduino 5V Pin
Electronics
  1. Set to V⎓, 20V range (or auto).
  2. Black probe to Arduino GND pin.
  3. Red probe to 5V pin.
  4. 4.75V – 5.25V is within spec. Outside this suggests regulator or USB power issue.
Example 3 — Car 12V Accessory Socket
Automotive
  1. Set to V⎓, 20V range.
  2. Ignition key to ACC or ON. Red probe to centre pin (hot), black to outer rim (ground).
  3. 11.5V – 14.5V = normal. Below 11V with key on = battery or wiring fault.
