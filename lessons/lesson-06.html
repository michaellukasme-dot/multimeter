Resistance (Ω)

Resistance tells you how much a component opposes current flow. Always measure with the circuit de-energized and the component isolated from parallel paths.

Ω
Ohms
Circuit must be powered off. Isolate component if possible to avoid parallel-path errors.

⚠
Power off first. Measuring on a live circuit gives false readings and can damage the meter. Discharge capacitors before probing in power supply circuits.

Example — Verifying a 10kΩ Resistor
Resistance
  1. Remove resistor from circuit (or fully de-power and isolate).
  2. Set to Ω, 20kΩ range.
  3. Probe across both leads — polarity doesn't matter.
  4. 5% tolerance: 9.5kΩ – 10.5kΩ
Example — Motor Winding Check
Resistance
  1. Disconnect motor from all power. Set to Ω, 200Ω range.
  2. Probe across the two motor terminals.
  3. 1Ω – 100Ω = healthy. OL = open winding (burned). 0Ω = shorted winding.
