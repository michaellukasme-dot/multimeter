Capacitance & Frequency

Advanced meters include capacitance (C in Farads) and frequency (Hz) modes — invaluable for checking timing circuits, capacitor health, and signal quality.

C (F)
Capacitance
Measures charge storage. Identifies failed or drifted capacitors. Discharge before testing.
nF / µF — discharge first!
Hz
Frequency
Cycles per second. Checks oscillators, PWM signals, mains frequency.
Up to 10MHz on most meters
⚠
Always discharge capacitors first! Large electrolytic caps (especially from power supplies) can hold dangerous charge. Short through a 10kΩ resistor for 5+ seconds before probing.
Example 1 — Verifying a 100µF Cap
Capacitance
  1. Remove cap, discharge through 10kΩ for 5 seconds.
  2. Set meter to µF or C mode.
  3. Red → positive (longer) lead, black → negative (shorter).
  4. 100µF ±20%: 80µF – 120µF. Below 50µF suggests aging or failure.
Example 2 — Mains Frequency Check
Frequency
  1. Set to Hz. Connect probes to AC outlet (same as V~ test).
  2. USA/Canada: 59.5Hz – 60.5Hz   Europe: 49.5Hz – 50.5Hz
  3. Significant deviation signals grid instability — important for sensitive lab equipment.
