Lab: Outlet & Wiring Tests

A multimeter is your primary tool for home electrical diagnostics. These tests identify dead outlets, reversed wiring, missing grounds, and high-resistance connections — without tearing into walls.

⚡
High voltage present. Use CAT III 600V minimum probes. Work with one hand when possible (keeps current from crossing your heart). Know where your breaker panel is before starting.

High-Resistance Connection Test
Lab Exercise

A connection can pass basic voltage tests but still have high resistance causing voltage drop under load (dim lights, slow motor, intermittent faults).

  1. Plug in a known load (lamp). Measure voltage at the outlet under load.
  2. Measure voltage at the breaker panel for the same circuit.
  3. Difference should be less than 3V. More than 5V difference = high-resistance connection in wiring or terminals.
  4. Switch to resistance mode (circuit OFF) and probe each terminal — connections should measure < 0.5Ω.
