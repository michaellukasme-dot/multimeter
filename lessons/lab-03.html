Lab: Finding Circuit Faults

Systematic fault-finding combines voltage, resistance, and continuity measurements. The three faults you'll encounter most are opens, shorts, and high-resistance connections.

OPEN
Open Circuit
Break in the path. No current flows. Continuity: silent. Resistance: OL.
Symptom: nothing works
SHORT
Short Circuit
Unintended low-R path. Voltage across it ≈ 0V. Resistance: near 0Ω.
Symptom: breaker trips
HiR
High Resistance
Corroded joint, loose terminal. Circuit works poorly. Voltage drop detected.
Symptom: dim, slow, intermittent
Half-Splitting Method (Open Circuit)
Procedure: Locating an Open in a Series Circuit
Fault Finding

Voltage appears before the open and disappears after it. Halve the circuit each probe placement to locate the fault efficiently.

  1. Set V⎓. Measure at source — confirm supply voltage is present.
  2. Probe midpoint. Full voltage → open is past this point. Zero → open is before it.
  3. Continue halving until voltage vanishes between two adjacent points.
  4. That segment contains the open — broken wire, failed component, or bad connection.
Procedure: Finding a Short to Ground
Fault Finding
  1. Remove fuse / turn off power. Set to continuity mode.
  2. Probe from each circuit node to chassis ground.
  3. A BEEP where there should be silence = short to ground at that node.
  4. Trace the wiring at that node for pinched insulation, solder bridges, or wet connector.
